# Customer Behavior RFM Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer transcation data to understand purchasing behavior and identify high-value customer segments. Using Python and RFM (Receny, Frequency, Monetary) framework, customers were segmented based on how recently the purchased, how often they purchase, and how much they spend.
The goal of this analysis was to move beyond basic descriptive statistics and uncover actionable insights that can support customer retention and revenue growth strategies.

## Business Objectives
- Identify high-value customers and understand their behavior
- Segment customers based on purchasing patterns
- Detect customers a risk of churn
- Evaluate how customer behavior impacts overall revenue
- Provide data-driven recommendations to improve retention and increase customer value

## Tools Used
- Python
- pandas (data cleaning, transformation, analysis)
- matplotlib (data visualization)
- Jupyter Notebook (via VS Code)

## Key Findings
- Customers were successfully segmented into Champions, Loyal Customers, At Risk, and Others using an RFM model
- Champion customers represent the smallest segment but have the highest total and average spending
- At Risk customers form a similarly sized group, indicating a significant number of customers are becoming disengaged
- Customer spending is highly skewed, with a small number of high-value customers contributing disproportionately to revenue
- Purchase frequency alone does not fully predict customer value due to high variablity in spending behavior

## Key Business Insights
- Revenue is highly concentrated within a small group of high-value customers (Champions)
- The presence of a similarly sized At Risk segments suggests potential future revenue loss if these customers are not re-engaged
- High-frequency customers are not a uniform group, some are consistently valuable, while others show irregular spending patterns
- Customer segmentation based on RFM provides a more accurate understanding of value than relying on a single metric like frequency

## Recommendations
- Retain Champion customers through loyalty programs, exclusive offers, and personalized engagement strategies
- Re-engage At Risk customers with targeted campaigns, incentives, and reminders to prevent churn
- Focus on converting low-frequency customers into repeat buyers through promotions or onboarding strategies
- Implement value-based segmentation in business decision-making rather than relying solely on purchase frequency
- Continuously monitor customer segments to detect behavioral shifts over time

## Conclusion
This analysis demonstrates that a small segment of customers drives a significant portion of revenue, while a nearly equal group of customers is at risk of disengagement. By applying RFM segmentation, businesses can better understand customer behavior, prioritize high-value segments, and take proactive steps to improve retention and long-term profitability.
