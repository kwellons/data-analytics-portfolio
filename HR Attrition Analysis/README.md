# HR Attrition Analysis | SQL & Power BI

## Project Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition trends to identify workforce groups with elevalted turnover risk and investigate operational and workforce factors that may contribute to employee turnover. Using SQL for data exploration and Power BI for visualization, the analysis focuses on uncovering actionable insights related to employee retention, compensation, overtime, and workforce stability. 

## Business Objectives
- Identify employee groups experiencing the highest attrition rates
- Evaluate whether overtime, compensation, and tenure influence turnover risk
- Analyze workfroce patterns across departments and job levels
- Detect high-risk employee segments through combined factor analysis
- Deliver actionable business recommendations supported by data insights

## Tools Used
- SQL (PostgreSQL / DBeaver)
- Power BI
- Excel / CSV Dataset

## Key Findings
- Employees working overtime experienced an attrition rate of **30.53%**, nearly three times higher than employees not working overtime
- Employees in lower income bands demonstrated significantly higher attrition rates (**21.76%**) compared to high-income groups (**8.9%**)
- Attrition was heavily concentrated among employees with **0-2 years** at the company, with turnover rates decreasing as tenture increased
- Entry-lvel employees (Job Level 1) experienced the highest attrition rate (**26.53%**) while senior-level employees demonstrated substantially lower turnover risk
- A high-risk employee segment consisting of low-tenure, overtime, and lower-income employees experienced an attrition rate exceeding **57%**

## Key Business Insights
- Workforce attrition appears to be strongly associated with workload pressure, compensation level, and early-stage employee retention challenges
- Overtime emerged as one of the strongest indicators of turnover risk within the organization
- The relationship between lower compensation and higher attrition suggests potential retention challenges among entry-level and lower-paid employees
- Combined workforce risk factors amplified attrition significantly more than individual variables analyzed independently
- Findings suggest that employee retention strategies should prioritize workforce stability during the early years of employment

## Recommendations
- Prioritize retention initiatives for early-career and lower-compensation employees
- Evaluate overtime distribution and workload management practices to reduce burnout-related turnover risk
- Review compensation competitiveness for entry-level roles and high-turnover department
- Strengthen onboarding, employee engagement, and manager support programs during employees' first years at the company
- Continue monitoring workforce trends through HR analytics dashboards and retention-focused KPI tracking

## Conclusion
This analysis demonstrates how SQL and Power BI can be used together to investigate workforce challenges, uncover high-risk employee segments, and support data-driven business decision-making. By combining exploratory SQL analysis with interactive BI storytelling, the project highlights the value of analytics in understanding employee behavior and improving organizational retention strategies
